Symmetry is not only about something looking balanced. A more interesting way to see it: symmetry is what survives a transformation.

Rotate a square by ninety degrees and it still behaves like the same square. Translate a pattern and the relationship between its parts can remain unchanged. Change the surface, and the structure may still persist.

That makes symmetry feel less like decoration and more like a test for identity: what can change while the important thing remains itself?
