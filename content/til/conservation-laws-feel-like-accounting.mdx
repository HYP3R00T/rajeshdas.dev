A conservation law feels powerful because it gives you an accounting system for reality.

Even when the motion is complicated, something is not allowed to vanish from the books. Energy may change form. Momentum may move between objects. Charge may relocate. But the total still has to reconcile.

That is why conservation laws are so useful for thinking. They let you ignore some local chaos and ask a better question: what must remain true after everything has changed?
